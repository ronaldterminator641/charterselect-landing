**Hook 1**

**Hook 2**
Fully insured: you pay a fixed premium, the insurer keeps the surplus.
That's the whole design. A good year never pays you back.
Schools your size already run on something better.
Here's what your broker never showed you: [LINK]

---

**Hook 3**
A level-funded plan splits your monthly payment three ways: claims fund, admin fee, stop-loss.
Stop-loss caps your downside. The surplus from a healthy year comes back as a refund.
Same budget predictability. Real upside.
How it actually works: [LINK]

**Hook 4**
One charter school is usually too small to self-fund alone.
A hundred charter schools that share a risk profile are not.
That's the whole idea behind a group captive.

**Hook 5**
Any broker who pitches alternative funding as free money is lying to you.
In a bad year you pay more, up to a ceiling you set in advance.
The point is a capped downside in exchange for the upside.

**Hook 6**
On a fully insured plan you get one number at renewal and no explanation.
