**Headline:** Switching carriers on a claims-made policy? Confirm these before you sign, or you may be paying full premium for a fraction of your coverage history.

When a charter school moves carriers on claims-made coverage (D&O, EPLI, educators' professional, abuse and molestation where applicable), one detail decides whether years of prior coverage survive the switch: the retroactive date. Advance it, and every event before the new policy's start date falls outside coverage. Here is how the three protection mechanisms compare, and the checklist we run on every claims-made renewal.

## The three ways to protect your coverage history

| Mechanism | What it does | Who provides it | When you use it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Preserved retroactive date** | New policy keeps your *original* retro date, so prior years stay covered | Incoming carrier | Standard ask on any carrier switch. Confirm in writing. |
| **Nose / Prior Acts coverage** | Incoming carrier agrees to cover events going back to your old retro date | Incoming carrier | When the new carrier won't simply match the original date |
| **Tail / Extended Reporting Period (ERP)** | Outgoing carrier lets you keep reporting prior-event claims after the policy ends | Outgoing carrier | When the incoming carrier won't cover prior acts at all |

You need at least one of these working every time a claims-made policy changes hands. Zero of them is the gap that erases your history.

## The renewal checklist

- [ ] Identify every claims-made policy in the program (don't assume; verify the form basis on each).
- [ ] Pull the current retroactive date on each claims-made policy, in writing.
- [ ] On any carrier switch, confirm the incoming retro date matches your original date, not the new inception date.
- [ ] If it can't match, confirm prior acts ("nose") coverage back to the original date.
- [ ] If nose isn't available, price and bind tail coverage (ERP) from the outgoing carrier before the old policy lapses.
- [ ] Read the declarations page and confirm the retro date in writing before binding.
- [ ] Document the confirmed retro date for the board record.

## Why this is standard practice for us and rare elsewhere

A generalist broker chasing a lower premium often sets the new policy up on the carrier's default terms, which advances the retro date silently. We renew early, read the full policy on every renewal, and confirm the retro date in writing every time, because the cost of getting this wrong lands on the exact exposure a school can least afford to lose.
