**Headline:** Have a School Resource Officer on campus? The two policies you carry most are usually written to exclude exactly the claim an officer creates.

The moment a sworn officer starts working your hallways, your school sits adjacent to law enforcement, and law enforcement carries a category of claim schools almost never see otherwise: use of force, wrongful arrest, improper search, detention a court later calls unreasonable. These get litigated as federal civil rights claims. Below is how your standard lines respond to that exposure, how the purpose-built line responds, and the checklist we run for any school with an officer on campus.

## How each policy line treats a law enforcement claim

| Coverage line | Built for | How it treats an SRO use-of-force / civil rights claim |
|---|---|---|
| **General Liability (GL)** | Ordinary bodily injury and property damage from operating a building | Law enforcement activity is a recognized professional exposure, commonly removed by a specific endorsement that excludes the named insured's law enforcement activities |
| **Educators Legal Liability (ELL)** | Wrongful acts tied to the educational job: failure to educate, discipline disputes, board and professional decisions | Claims arising from intentional or criminal acts, the territory of a force or false-arrest claim, often sit outside the policy |
| **Law Enforcement Legal Liability (LELL)** | Claims arising from law enforcement activities | Responds to excessive force, wrongful arrest or detention, improper search and seizure, and civil rights (1983) claims; typically funds defense costs. Note its own carve-outs: willful/criminal conduct and punitive damages are commonly excluded |

GL and ELL are each drafted to push law enforcement claims away. A use-of-force suit lands in the seam between them. LELL is the line engineered to catch it, and it is usually not riding along inside the policies you already have.

## The SRO coverage checklist

- [ ] Confirm whether a sworn officer works any of your campuses (department-assigned or school-employed).
- [ ] Locate the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for any department-assigned officer and read it in full.
- [ ] In the MOU, identify the indemnity language: who indemnifies whom, and for whose conduct.
- [ ] In the MOU, identify any requirement that the school carry insurance for this exposure, and at what limit.
- [ ] Pull your GL form and confirm whether a law enforcement activities exclusion endorsement is attached.
- [ ] Pull your ELL form and confirm how it treats intentional and criminal acts.
- [ ] Determine whether Law Enforcement Legal Liability (LELL / police professional) coverage is in place anywhere in the program.
- [ ] If LELL is required by the MOU or by the exposure and is absent, price and place it before the next incident, not after.
- [ ] Confirm the MOU's insurance obligations are actually backed by bound coverage, in writing.
- [ ] Document the confirmed structure for the board record.

## Why this is standard practice for us and rare elsewhere

A broker who treats charter schools as small accounts to renew quickly does not read your MOU, was not at the table when it was signed, and renews your GL and ELL without a thought about the law enforcement exclusion sitting inside both. We ask whether there is an officer on campus on the first call, read the MOU against your policies, and confirm whether the line built for this exposure is actually in force, because a federal civil rights claim is the worst possible moment to discover the seam.
