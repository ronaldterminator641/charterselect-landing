**Headline:** Before you accept another fully insured renewal, understand what the model does with your money in a good year and a bad one.

The plan most charter schools carry is fully insured: a fixed monthly premium, with the insurer taking on all claims risk. It looks simple and predictable. The structural problem is who keeps the surplus when your staff stays healthy, and who absorbs the increase when they don't. Here is how the fully insured model treats your money against the alternative funding approach the model leaves out, and the checklist we run before any school accepts a renewal.

## How the funding model treats your money

| Scenario | Fully insured | Alternative funding |
|---|---|---|
| **Good claims year (low claims)** | Insurer keeps the surplus. No refund, no credit, no notice. | Surplus can come back into your budget instead of the insurer's. |
| **Bad claims year (high claims)** | Insurer covers claims, then raises your rate at renewal to recover and protect margin. | Defined structure for high-claim years, reviewed with real numbers and honest tradeoffs. |
| **Claims data visibility** | Almost none. You get a renewal number with no window into how it was built. | Usable claims data, so you can see what is actually driving cost. |
| **Cost management** | Treated as a fixed cost you absorb. | Treated as a spend you can analyze and manage. |
| **Who carries the risk position** | You carry the downside in both directions, capture the upside in neither. | Designed so a healthy workforce can work in your favor. |

The fully insured arrangement is the one where a school is guaranteed to never benefit from its own people's good health. Alternative funding models are not reserved for large corporations; schools of this size use them. They carry their own structure and tradeoffs, which is a separate analysis, not a default switch.

## The renewal review checklist

- [ ] Confirm your current funding model in writing (fully insured, level-funded, or other).
- [ ] Ask where the surplus went the last time your staff had a healthy claims year.
- [ ] Request your claims experience for the plan year, not just the renewal number.
- [ ] Identify the top cost drivers (large claims, pharmacy spend, emergency room overuse) before accepting any increase.
- [ ] Have the renewal increase explained against actual claims, not "the market."
- [ ] Get the alternative funding models laid out with real numbers for your group, including their honest tradeoffs.
- [ ] Compare what the program costs you against what those dollars do elsewhere in the building (staff, counselors, retention).
- [ ] Document the funding decision and the rationale for the board record.

## Why this is standard practice for us and rare elsewhere

A generalist broker renews a school fully insured every year because it is the easy button. No data to pull, no analysis to run, no alternatives to explain. They re-up the same plan, pass along the increase, and call it service. We treat your health spend as something you can manage, walk you through where your premium dollars went, and put the alternatives on the table with real numbers, because for a school every premium dollar is a dollar with somewhere better to be.
