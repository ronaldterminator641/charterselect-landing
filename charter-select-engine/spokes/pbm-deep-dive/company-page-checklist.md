**Headline:** Your drug benefit runs on a PBM contract you have probably never read. Here is how to tell whether it is built for your budget or the middleman's.

Every prescription your plan covers is priced by a Pharmacy Benefit Manager. Two PBMs can charge wildly different amounts for the identical drug, and the difference is the contract structure, not the medication. Below is how a transparent pass-through model compares to a traditional spread-and-rebate model on the points that decide your spend, followed by the review we run on every pharmacy benefit.

## Transparent pass-through vs traditional spread-and-rebate PBM

| Lever | Traditional spread-and-rebate PBM | Transparent pass-through PBM |
|---|---|---|
| **How the PBM gets paid** | Spread, retained rebates, and per-claim fees, so revenue rises with your spend | One flat, disclosed fee per member per month |
| **Drug pricing to your plan** | Marked up above what the pharmacy was paid; you see one number | Exactly what the pharmacy was paid, using proxies like NADAC plus a fixed dispensing fee |
| **Manufacturer rebates** | A share is retained by the PBM | 100% passed through to the plan |
| **Formulary incentive** | Can favor the high-list-price drug that pays the bigger rebate | Steered to lowest net cost, favoring generics and biosimilars |
| **Hidden charges** | Clawbacks, DIR fees, admin fees that balloon with surcharges | Disclosed; no spread, no per-claim cut to hide |
| **The scoreboard** | Biggest rebate | Lowest net cost (total spend minus every rebate, per member per month) |
| **Member visibility** | Black-box claims and prior-auth status | Claims you can see, pharmacy price-finder, trackable prior-auth |

A traditional structure profits when your drug spend goes up. A pass-through structure makes the same flat fee whether your people fill more scripts or fewer, which removes the reason to steer them toward the expensive option.

## The pharmacy benefit checklist

- [ ] Confirm whether your PBM is a transparent pass-through or a traditional spread-and-rebate model.
- [ ] Ask, in writing, for the difference between what the pharmacy was paid and what your plan was billed on a sample of claims.
- [ ] Confirm what share of manufacturer rebates flows back to the plan, and get the number in writing.
- [ ] Total every administrative and ad-hoc fee in the contract, not just the headline PMPM rate.
- [ ] Ask how the formulary is built: lowest net cost, or biggest rebate.
- [ ] Confirm the PBM is paid a flat per-member fee, not a per-claim fee that rewards more transactions.
- [ ] On specialty drugs, confirm the program runs copay assistance, patient assistance programs (PAPs), and any sourcing levers inside real guardrails, not as a free lunch.
- [ ] Establish a lowest-net-cost PMPM figure you can actually watch quarter to quarter.

## Why this is standard practice for us

A generalist broker inherits the PBM bundled inside the carrier's fully insured plan, renews it every year, and never opens the pharmacy contract. We carve the pharmacy benefit out, put a transparent pass-through PBM behind it, hold the program to a lowest-net-cost standard, and layer in the specialty levers where they fit and where they are safe.
