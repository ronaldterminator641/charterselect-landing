**Headline:** Your insurance program almost certainly bundles in free services you are already funding. Here is how to find them, turn them on, and stop paying for a toolbox nobody opened.

School and nonprofit insurance programs rarely stop at coverage. Carriers that specialize in this space bundle in value-added services, and a portion of their cost is already built into your premium. The problem is activation, not availability. The list below maps the services that commonly ride along with a program, who delivers each one, and what it costs you to leave it dormant. Treat it as a menu to ask about, not a promise that every item is on your policy.

## What tends to be in the box, and the cost of leaving it closed

| Value-add | What it does for you | Who provides it | Cost of leaving it dormant |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HR / employment-law hotline** | Attorney or HR guidance before you discipline, demote, or fire | Carrier or program partner | Paying outside counsel retail for calls you could make free |
| **Risk-management portal** | Checklists, safety guides, incident tracking | Carrier or program admin | A login that dies in an inbox and a safety library nobody reads |
| **Online training library** | Harassment, mandated-reporter, FERPA courses with completion tracking | Carrier or program partner | No documentation of training when a regulator or plaintiff asks |
| **Background-screening discount** | Reduced rates on staff and volunteer checks | Negotiated screening vendor | Paying retail for checks you already qualify to get cheaper |
| **Loss-control / site visit** | A risk pro walks your facility and flags hazards before claims | Carrier | Preventable claims that drive next year's premium up |
| **Employee assistance program (EAP)** | Confidential counseling and referral for staff | Benefits carrier or program | A benefit your staff never use because nobody told them |
| **Telemedicine** | Virtual clinician access for routine staff issues | Health plan or standalone | Staff in urgent care for things a video call could handle |
| **Cyber breach-response hotline** | The number you call in the first hour of a suspected breach | Cyber carrier | A worse claim outcome from a slow or wrong first move |

You are unlikely to have every line. You almost certainly have more of it than you are using.

## The perks activation checklist

- [ ] Ask your broker or carrier for a written inventory of every value-added service on every policy (property, liability, management liability, cyber, benefits).
- [ ] For each portal or online tool, get the live login URL, the credentials, and the name of who holds the master account.
- [ ] Reset any credentials that trace back to a staffer who has left.
- [ ] Put every hotline number (HR, breach-response, EAP) on one sheet and decide in advance who is authorized to call.
- [ ] Compare the program's background-screening rates to what you pay now, and claim the discount if there is a gap.
- [ ] Request the loss-control site visit on purpose rather than waiting to be offered it.
- [ ] Assign each live service a named owner so someone is responsible for working it during the year.
- [ ] Announce the staff-facing perks (EAP, telemedicine) at a meeting, in the handbook, and at open enrollment.
- [ ] Re-audit the full list at every renewal, since programs quietly add and drop services.

## Why this is standard practice for us and rare elsewhere

A generalist broker sells these services once, in the proposal, as bullet points that make the program look richer, then never onboards them. Activating a portal or circulating a hotline number does not pay the broker, and the time spent doing it on a small account is time a commodity broker would rather spend on a bigger one. We hand charter schools the inventory of included services without being asked, confirm the logins work, walk leadership through the hotlines, schedule the loss-control visit, and show what changed in the bundle at every renewal. That is not heroic. It is onboarding the thing you already bought.
