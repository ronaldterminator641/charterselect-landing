# Company Page Spoke: Modern Open Enrollment Readiness Checklist

**Headline:** If your open enrollment is one binder and one meeting, you are paying full premium for benefits your staff don't understand and don't use.

Benefit spend is one of the three largest line items a charter school carries. The way enrollment gets run decides whether that money lands with your staff or lands with a thud. Below is how the annual scramble compares to enrollment run as a year-round process, and the checklist we work through before every renewal season.

## Annual scramble vs. enrollment run as a process

| Element | The annual scramble | Enrollment run as a process | Why the gap costs you |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enrollment mechanics** | Paper forms hand-keyed into a carrier portal | Benefits admin platform with eligibility rules enforced at entry | Manual entry is where eligibility errors, missed deadlines, and bad dependent records start |
| **Plan selection** | One binder, one slide deck, default to last year's box | Decision support tailored to each employee's situation | A new parent and a near-retiree default into the same plan that fits neither |
| **Communication** | A single meeting at the end of a faculty day | Multi-format plan with runway before the deadline | People can't engage with a choice they were given no time to consider |
| **After enrollment** | Nothing | Education on how to actually use the coverage | A benefit nobody uses is a benefit nobody values |
| **Compliance** | Exposure from rushed manual data entry | Clean data flowing straight to carriers | Rushed entry is the root of most enrollment compliance risk |

You don't need every element perfect. You need the process pointed in the right direction before your season starts, not a binder dropped on a desk.

## The pre-enrollment readiness checklist

- [ ] Confirm whether elections run through a benefits administration platform or still move on paper.
- [ ] Verify the platform enforces eligibility rules at the point of entry, not after the fact.
- [ ] Confirm clean election data flows to carriers without anyone retyping it.
- [ ] Put decision-support tools in front of every employee so the choice fits the person, not the average.
- [ ] Build a communication calendar that starts weeks before the deadline, in formats staff will actually open.
- [ ] Include a plain-language one-page summary, a live session, and an email sequence in that calendar.
- [ ] Give staff enough runway to take the choice home and discuss it before deciding.
- [ ] Schedule post-enrollment education on telehealth, pharmacy benefits, and no-cost preventive care.
- [ ] Confirm who owns each of these steps on your account, in writing, before the season opens.

## Why this is standard practice for us and rare elsewhere

A generalist broker drops off a binder, sits in on one meeting, processes the renewal, and calls it service. That takes an afternoon. Building enrollment that runs all year takes work across the whole year, and a broker who treats a school as too small to fuss over won't do it. We stand up the platform, build the communication calendar, put decision support in front of every employee, and come back after the deadline to teach people how to use what they picked, because your benefit spend should land with your staff instead of leaving them resentful of a benefit you paid full price for.
