**Headline:** A health plan is not a strategy. Here is the full stack we assemble for a charter school, layer by layer, plus the support layer that makes it actually work.

Most charter schools are handed one carrier, one network, one pharmacy manager, all welded together and sold as a single package. The schools that spend less and care better stop buying that bundle and start assembling a stack, where every piece is chosen on its own merits, then wrapped in a support layer a regular teacher can use without a benefits degree. Here is the full build and the readiness checklist we run before anyone talks about switching a plan.

## The stack, layer by layer

| Layer | What it does | The piece we assemble | Why it beats the bundle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Funding base** | Decides who keeps the savings in a good claims year | Level-funded or self-funded plan with stop-loss, on a path toward a captive | Savings stay with the school instead of padding a carrier's margin; a captive pools risk so no single school eats a brutal year alone |
| **Independent PBM** | Runs the drug benefit | Transparent, pass-through PBM, carved out and chosen on its own | Disclosed fee, real drug cost passed through, rebates returned to your plan, instead of spread pricing and retained rebates |
| **RBP and price transparency** | Sets fair payment limits on facility and provider bills | Reference-based pricing benchmarked to a public standard, with transparency tools | Caps payment against a public benchmark instead of a hospital's mystery chargemaster; published savings often run 15 to 30 percent versus traditional PPO claims |
| **Unbundled TPA + rented network** | Processes claims, runs the plan, provides provider access | Independent TPA (carrier- and hospital-independent) plus a rented national network | The administrator answers to the plan, not a parent insurer; you keep plan-design control, real claims data, and the ability to plug in outside vendors |
| **Accountable-care design** | Points members toward the right care by default | Primary-care-anchored design with free, coordinated primary care | Free front-door primary care gets used more, catching problems early; reported savings commonly 20 percent or more versus traditional carriers |
| **MSP and COBRA guardrails** | Protects the fund from rules a carrier used to absorb | Active Medicare Secondary Payer order-of-payment management and deliberate COBRA pricing against stop-loss | On a self-funded plan, a wrong Medicare order or unmanaged COBRA adverse selection drains the fund and carries penalties; somebody has to stand guard |
| **Member support and navigation** | Protects the human being in the hardest moments | Oncology-support program plus care navigation and advocacy | A real person and sub-specialist cancer expertise on call; steerage matters when the same procedure can vary by up to 400 percent in price in one town |

## The assembly and readiness checklist

- [ ] Confirm the funding base: level-funded or self-funded with appropriate stop-loss, and map the path to a captive as headcount grows.
- [ ] Carve out the pharmacy benefit to a transparent, pass-through PBM with disclosed fees and rebates returned to the plan.
- [ ] Pair a rented national network with reference-based pricing and live price-transparency tools.
- [ ] Place administration with a carrier- and hospital-independent TPA; confirm direct access to your own claims data.
- [ ] Anchor the plan design on coordinated primary care; confirm low or zero member cost at the point of primary care.
- [ ] Set the Medicare Secondary Payer order of payment correctly for active employees 65+, and re-verify it as headcount crosses the 20-employee threshold.
- [ ] Track COBRA continuants, price COBRA so the fund is not silently subsidizing it, and structure stop-loss (including lasers where appropriate) for known high-cost claimants at renewal.
- [ ] Stand up the member-support layer: an oncology-support benefit and a care-navigation and advocacy line, plugged into the TPA.
- [ ] Put the consumerism tools directly in members' hands: the navigation number, the transparency tool, and decision support, delivered at open enrollment in formats people actually open.
