**Headline:** Before you renew the same network, see how its "discount" actually stacks up against the published cash price. On the high-volume services, the gap is often the wrong direction.

Hospital and facility spend is the single heaviest line in almost every health plan, and since 2021 the prices behind it have been public and required by law. Most plans never get the comparison run. Here is how the four pricing reference points relate to each other, and the review we run on a plan's highest-volume shoppable services before recommending a single change.

## The four numbers in play on any procedure

| Price reference | What it is | How it is set | What to do with it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chargemaster / gross charge** | The hospital's list price | Set high on purpose as an opening position; not tied to delivery cost | Treat as theater. The number every "discount" is measured against. |
| **Negotiated (network) rate** | What your PPO negotiated | A percentage off the chargemaster | Verify it against the cash price; a discount off a rigged number can still run high. |
| **Cash / self-pay price** | What the hospital posts for no-insurance payers | Published per the Hospital Price Transparency rule | Compare to the negotiated rate on your top services. Lower in roughly 47% of cases. |
| **Transparent benchmark (e.g., Medicare-referenced)** | A cost-based anchor | Tied to a public reference, not a chargemaster | The basis for reference-based pricing if the data supports a move. |

You cannot tell whether your network is buying lower prices or selling access to higher ones until those numbers sit side by side. Renewing without that comparison is renewing blind on your biggest line.

## The pre-renewal review checklist

- [ ] Identify the plan's highest-volume shoppable services (imaging, common labs, routine outpatient procedures).
- [ ] List the facilities your staff actually use, not a generic regional average.
- [ ] Pull each facility's published cash price for those services from its machine-readable transparency file.
- [ ] Pull the plan's negotiated rate for the same service, same facility, same setting.
- [ ] Flag every service where the cash price is at or below the negotiated rate.
- [ ] Quantify annual plan exposure: per-service gap multiplied by utilization.
- [ ] Confirm price is not standing in for quality (price-quality correlation is weak; do not assume the high number buys a better outcome).
- [ ] Decide where network access earns its cost (catastrophic and complex care) versus where transparent cash-level pricing wins (high-volume shoppable services).
- [ ] If the data supports it, evaluate reference-based pricing as one downstream tool, scoped in a separate analysis.
- [ ] Document the comparison for the board record before binding the renewal.
