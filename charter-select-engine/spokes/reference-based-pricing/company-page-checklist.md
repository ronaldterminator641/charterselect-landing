**Headline:** Your network discount is calculated off a price the hospital invented. Here is what it looks like to pay off a real benchmark instead.

Hospital and facility spend is the heaviest line in almost every charter school health plan. A PPO network discounts that spend off the hospital's chargemaster, a list price set high on purpose and not tied to the cost of care. Reference-based pricing (RBP) starts from a transparent Medicare benchmark and pays up from there. Here is how the two pricing models compare, and the checklist we run before recommending any move.

## Network pricing vs. reference-based pricing

| Factor | PPO network plan | Reference-based pricing |
|---|---|---|
| **Starting point** | Hospital's inflated chargemaster list price | Transparent Medicare benchmark |
| **How the price is set** | A discount negotiated off the list price | A defined multiple of Medicare, often 120 to 200 percent |
| **Typical result** | Frequently several times Medicare, sometimes 300 percent or more | A fair, sustainable price well below network levels |
| **Required chassis** | Fully insured or self-funded | Self-funded or level-funded only |
| **Main risk** | Overpaying quietly, year after year | Balance billing, managed by member advocacy and legal defense |
| **Reported savings** | Trimming the edges leaves the heaviest line untouched | Industry estimates commonly cite 20 to 30 percent of total plan spend |

You cannot run RBP on a fully insured policy. The insurer sets the price and keeps the upside, so there is no lever to pull. RBP requires a self-funded chassis where the plan, meaning your school, defines its allowable amount per claim.

## The renewal checklist

- [ ] Confirm the plan is self-funded or level-funded; RBP cannot bolt onto a fully insured policy.
- [ ] Pull your largest hospital and facility claims from last year and benchmark each against the Medicare rate.
- [ ] Compare what the plan actually paid to what the same service costs at a defined multiple of Medicare.
- [ ] Confirm the proposed RBP multiple in writing (commonly 120 to 200 percent of Medicare by service).
- [ ] Verify the balance-billing defense: member advocacy plus a patient-side legal team named in the contract, not promised verbally.
- [ ] Confirm the employee communication plan before launch, including who staff call the moment a balance bill arrives.
- [ ] Treat any single savings percentage as a marketing number until someone runs your real claims data.
