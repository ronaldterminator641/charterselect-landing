**Headline:** Most charter schools renew the same fully insured plan every year and never see the two upgrades that deliver better care for less. Here is the comparison, and the checklist we run before your next renewal.

Charter schools recruit against districts with pensions and deeper salary bands. The one place you can compete is the total package, and health benefits sit at the center of it. The standard fully insured plan, renewed every year with a higher premium, delivers slow, shallow care at a rising price. Two upgrades change both the care your staff receives and the dollars your school spends: Direct Primary Care wrapped with an insurance layer behind it, and a transparent pass-through pharmacy model. Here is how the standard setup compares, and the design checklist we run on every benefits renewal.

## The standard plan versus a designed benefit

| Dimension | Standard fully insured plan | Direct Primary Care + transparent Rx |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary care access** | Seven-minute visits, appointments weeks out, large patient panels | Longer visits, same-day or next-day scheduling, direct messaging, small panels |
| **How primary care is paid** | Per-visit billing that rewards volume | Flat monthly membership fee per member, unlimited access |
| **Routine labs and generics** | Billed through insurance, member pays toward deductible | Often in-house labs and wholesale-cost generics, little or nothing at the counter |
| **Pharmacy pricing** | Hidden spread, manufacturer rebates retained by the PBM | Pass-through pricing, disclosed admin fee, rebates flow back to the plan |
| **Insurance layer** | Carries all care, including high-frequency routine visits | Leaner layer behind DPC for specialists, surgery, and catastrophic events |
| **Cost trajectory** | Premium creeps up every renewal | Front-end access offsets a cheaper insurance layer; spend often bends down |

DPC is not insurance and does not replace it. It handles the routine, high-frequency care. The wrapped insurance plan stands behind it for everything else. You need both halves built and fitted together, which is design work, not a renewal.

## The benefit design checklist

- [ ] Pull how your staff actually uses care: primary care visit volume, lab spend, top recurring prescriptions.
- [ ] Identify where front-end access is failing your people (wait times, rushed visits, urgent care and ER usage that primary care should have caught).
- [ ] Evaluate Direct Primary Care practices that serve your geography and vet panel size, access model, in-house labs, and dispensing.
- [ ] Confirm how the DPC membership wraps with the insurance layer, and right-size that layer so front-end access offsets a leaner plan.
- [ ] Audit your current PBM contract for spread pricing and rebate retention.
- [ ] Price a transparent, pass-through pharmacy model: pharmacy-cost billing, a disclosed admin fee, rebates flowing back to the plan.
- [ ] Model the combined cost of DPC plus transparent Rx plus the wrapped insurance against your current renewal.
- [ ] Document the design and the projected member experience for the board record.
